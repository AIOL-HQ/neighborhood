## What Is This?

The AIOL Neighborhood is the most ambitious project under the [AIOL umbrella](https://github.com/aiol-hq) — a real, physical, intentional community in Al-Karak, Jordan. Not a smart city. Not a tech campus. A **home** — designed for privacy, self-sufficiency, and genuine community, built from the ground up with the same principles that guide every other AIOL project.

This repository documents the vision, planning, infrastructure design, and roadmap for the neighborhood as it develops from idea to reality.

## The Land

| | |
|---|---|
| **Total Area** | 200 Dunums (~50 acres / ~200,000 m²) |
| **Shared Infrastructure** | 60 Dunums — streets, green areas, community services |
| **Residential Land** | 140 Dunums — for homes and private farms |
| **Estimated Units** | 40–70 homes |

The 60 dunums of shared space will include:

- Underground electrical cables
- Underground water pipes and sewage systems
- Underground fiber optic internet backbone
- Green areas and streets

This is infrastructure that simply doesn't exist in most of Al-Karak today — built right, built to last.

## The Community

### Residency

Residency is **selective**. Residents are chosen by a **community vote** — not a landlord, not a committee, not a corporation. The existing community decides who joins.

**Residency criteria include:**

1. Must be Muslim
2. Must have no ongoing conflicts with other residents or their families
3. Must be working and aiming to marry / already married
4. Agreement to community values (see below)
5. Commitment to privacy, FOSS principles, and community contribution

This isn't exclusion for its own sake — it's intentional design for a close-knit, conflict-free community built to last generations.

### Community Values

- **Privacy** — your data, your home, your life. No surveillance within the community.
- **FOSS** — open source is the default. Proprietary lock-in has no place here.
- **Contribution** — every resident adds something. Code, labor, knowledge, or care.
- **Self-Sufficiency** — the goal is a community that can sustain itself independently.

## Housing & Land Model

You don't buy land — you **rent it from AIOL** on a 10-year renewable basis. Here's how it works:

1. You reach an agreement with AIOL on a home blueprint
2. AIOL builds the structure at low cost, with a modest profit margin
3. You move in and decorate / furnish entirely as you choose
4. AIOL handles the base farm setup, water tanks, and solar energy installation
5. Land lease is **~15,000–20,000 JD per 10 years** (renewable)

This keeps land ownership unified under the community structure while keeping entry costs realistic for ordinary Jordanian families — not investors.

---

## The Farm

Every home includes a **mandatory small farm** — not optional, not decorative.

Food self-sufficiency is a core pillar of the neighborhood's resilience model. If the supply chain breaks down, the neighborhood eats. The base farm setup is included in the initial build.

## The Network

### Internet

| | |
|---|---|
| **Target Speed** | 500 Mbps per home |
| **Target Price** | ~22 JD/month (vs. ~30 JD market rate) |
| **Optional Bundle** | +2 JD/month for extra local services |
| **Upstream Provider** | ZainJO (via AIOL's own servers) |

### Local Services (Neighborhood-Wide Intranet)

The neighborhood runs its own private local network with the full AIOL stack:

| Service | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Private AI Server** | Local LLM — no cloud, no tracking |
| **Najim** | Home assistant, integrated with Home Assistant |
| **Hakeem Home** | Personal health & wellness AI |
| **Jellyfin** | Local media streaming |
| **KIWIX** | Offline knowledge base (Wikipedia, etc.) |
| **Pi-hole** | Network-wide ad blocking and DNS filtering |
| **Soulseek / Swing Music** | Local music library |
| **Home Assistant** | Smart home automation, connected to central AI |

### Air-Gap Mode

The entire network is **manually air-gappable** — meaning the neighborhood can be disconnected from the internet entirely and continue to function normally. All services remain available. Local communication stays up. The community becomes self-contained.

This isn't a disaster mode — it's a design philosophy. The neighborhood is built so that the internet is a *convenience*, not a dependency.
